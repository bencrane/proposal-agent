# Pricing Models

## Core Philosophy
We sell outcomes, not services. Pricing reflects the value of the outcome to the client, not the cost of our labor. The model varies by client, industry, and relationship dynamics.

## General Structure
Most deals follow: **Initial Fee + Per Primary Outcome**

### Initial Fee (Setup / Infrastructure / Investment)
- Covers: dedicated sending infrastructure, data sourcing, campaign strategy, creative assets
- Typical range: $2,500 – $10,000
- Framing matters: "setup fee" for SMBs, "infrastructure investment" for enterprise, "engagement fee" for PE-backed

### Performance Fee (Per Primary Outcome)
- Booked meeting: $100 – $500 depending on industry and deal value
- Sample requested (CPG/spirits): varies by product cost
- Live transfer: $50 – $200
- Scheduled callback: $30 – $100

### Monthly Retainer
- Used when: the client's industry normalizes monthly fees (staffing, recruiting, SaaS)
- Avoid when: selling to PE firms or executives where monthly = vendor status signal
- Typical range: $5,000 – $10,000/mo

## Model Variants

### setup_plus_performance (Default)
Setup fee + pay per outcome. Best for performance-oriented conversations.
- Pro: aligns incentives, easy to justify
- Con: requires confidence in campaign performance

### setup_plus_monthly
Setup fee + ongoing monthly. Traditional agency model.
- Pro: predictable revenue
- Con: feels like a vendor relationship for some buyers

### flat_project
One-time fixed fee for a defined scope (e.g., "$27,500 for 90-day campaign").
- Pro: simple, clean, no ongoing negotiation
- Con: less flexible, risk on our side

### pilot_to_contract
Low/free pilot to validate → larger commitment.
- Example: "Free 5-day trial (you pay $500) → if we validate, $25,000 for 500 outcomes"
- Pro: removes risk for buyer, builds trust, unlocks larger upfront payment
- Con: costs us time/money upfront
- Best for: skeptical buyers, new verticals, friends/warm intros

### custom
Any blend. Used when the conversation requires something non-standard.

## Pricing Signals by Buyer Type

| Buyer Type | Preferred Model | Status Signal |
|-----------|----------------|---------------|
| PE-backed company | Flat project or performance | Monthly = vendor, bad |
| Staffing agency | Monthly retainer | Normal, expected |
| SMB/startup | Setup + performance | Align incentives |
| Enterprise | Flat project | Budget line item |
| CPG/spirits brand | Pilot → contract | Needs validation first |
| Friend/warm intro | Pilot → contract | Build trust, then scale |

## Cash Flow Considerations
- When cash flow is tight: prefer upfront setup + deposits on performance
- When cash flow is fine: can absorb pilot costs to unlock bigger deals
- Performance deals may require upfront deposits depending on projected volume
