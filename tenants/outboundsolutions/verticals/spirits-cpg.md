# Spirits / CPG Vertical Context

## Primary Outcome
Sample requests from on-premise decision-makers (bar managers, beverage directors, F&B directors).

## The Market
- ~50,000 people in the US with direct authority over beverage purchasing and menu selection
- Titles: Beverage Director, Beverage Manager, Director of F&B, VP Culinary & Beverage, VP Procurement
- Industries: Hospitality, Restaurants, Hotels, Entertainment, Country Clubs, Casinos

## The Offer
The strongest offer in this vertical is a free sample. Getting the product into the hands of the decision-maker before the sales conversation is the highest-leverage move. Most campaigns don't have an offer this good.

A landing page is built during the Build phase — branded to the client, optimized for conversion. Prospects submit their info to receive a free sample. Every submission is captured, timestamped, and surfaced for fulfillment.

## Pricing Tendency
- Pilot → contract is natural here (validate the offer works, then scale)
- Example: "Free 5-day trial ($500) → $25,000 for 500 sample requests"
- Or: $5,000 setup + $7,500/mo for 90-day engagement
- Sample cost is on the client (typically ~$30/box) — most economical way to get tasted

## Common Pain Points
- Founders driving bar-to-bar (low thousands per trip + massive opportunity cost)
- Distributors carry hundreds of brands, allocate time accordingly
- Hiring a rep: $8–10K/mo fully loaded, still needs to source own leads
- Paid B2B meetings: $2–3K per meeting

## Key Messaging
- "The constraint is not demand. It is discovery."
- "The sample removes the guesswork."
- Position the engagement as putting the product on the bar in every market they enter.
