# Proposal Structure — 11 Sections

Every proposal follows this exact structure. Some sections are mostly static (Our Approach, The Engagement, Your Role, Next Steps). Others are fully dynamic and personalized.

## 01 — Executive Summary
2-3 sentences. What we're going to do, for whom, and the goal. Should feel like a confident handshake.
Example tone: "This proposal outlines the GTM strategy to put [Brand] in front of thousands of [target persona] each month..."

## 02 — The Opportunity
Why NOW is the right time. What's going well for them. Momentum signals from the call. The constraint they're hitting (usually: discovery, reach, scale).

## 03 — The Assessment
Their current approach and why it's inefficient. Cost of the status quo. Be specific — use details from the call. This section should make them feel the pain. Include cost comparisons (hiring in-house vs. us, paid ads vs. us, etc.)

## 04 — Our Approach
Mostly static. We are an engineering firm. We build infrastructure, not templates. Every variable is measurable and tunable. The firms that win at outbound are the ones that can launch, test, and adapt faster.

## 05 — The Market
TAM preview. How many prospects exist in their target market. Show a preview table of real leads we'd go after. (Populated by the prospecting sub-agent — use placeholder if not available.)

## 06 — The Offer
What the campaign actually looks like. What the prospect/their customers will experience. If there's a landing page or sample offer, describe it here. If there's a trial/pilot, describe the pilot structure.

## 07 — The Math
Projected numbers: email volume, response rate, close rate, revenue per account, total new accounts, first-year revenue, cost per account, ROI multiple. Use conservative assumptions. Flag that projections are illustrative.

## 08 — The Engagement
Three phases: Build (weeks 1-3), Launch (ongoing), Optimize (continuous). Describe what happens in each. This is mostly templated but can be adjusted per engagement.

## 09 — Pricing & Terms
Clear, simple. Show the price structure: one-time + ongoing (or whatever model). No hidden costs. Total clearly stated.

## 10 — Your Role
What the client needs to do on their end. Keep it short and specific. Usually: take the meetings, fulfill samples, respond quickly, have inventory/capacity ready.

## 11 — Next Steps
Signature block + payment instructions. Standard across all proposals.
