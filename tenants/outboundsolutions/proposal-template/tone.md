# Tone & Voice Guide

## Overall Voice
- Confident but not arrogant
- Direct — no fluff, no filler, no jargon
- Engineering-minded — we build systems, not campaigns
- Numbers-driven — show the math, show the data
- Respectful of the prospect's intelligence — don't oversell

## What We Sound Like
- "We built this." (not "We can help you with...")
- "The constraint is not demand. It is discovery."
- "Our outbound infrastructure is engineered to..." (not "We offer cold email services")
- "Every variable is measurable and tunable."

## What We DON'T Sound Like
- No marketing-speak ("leverage synergies", "unlock potential")
- No begging ("we'd love the opportunity to...")
- No hedging ("we think we might be able to...")
- No generic claims ("best-in-class", "world-class", "cutting-edge")

## Formatting
- Short paragraphs. 2-3 sentences max.
- Bold key statements for scannability.
- Use specific numbers wherever possible.
- Headers are clean and descriptive.

## Proposal-Specific Notes
- The Assessment section can be the most direct/aggressive — make them feel the cost of the status quo
- The Executive Summary should be the most polished — it's the first thing they read
- Pricing section should be clean and unambiguous — no confusion about what they're paying
